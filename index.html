Effervesce: an outcome of the SODA & GET:ORG research projects

Developed collaboratively by Tampere University, University of Helsinki, and University of Jyväskylä, Effervesce investigates how multi‑agent LLMs can facilitate balanced participation, knowledge grounding, and decision capture in digital workspaces.

About this research outcome

Effervesce is a practical outcome emerging from ongoing academic work on AI‑mediated organizational communication. It demonstrates how multi‑agent LLMs can support multi‑party conversations in Slack and Microsoft Teams by coordinating facilitation, retrieval, and note‑taking.

Lineage: projects & context

Effervesce is developed under two research projects at the University of Helsinki and partner institutions:

  • SODA — Social Data Science and Digitalization: blogs.helsinki.fi/sodaproject/
  • GET:ORG — Generative Technologies in Communicative Organizing: researchportal.helsinki.fi/.../getorg

Effervesce is not a standalone product; it is a research outcome illustrating methods, tooling, and evaluation practices from these projects.

Research approach

  • Experimenting with multi‑agent orchestration to distribute conversational tasks.
  • Using retrieval‑augmented generation (RAG) and knowledge graphs to improve contextual awareness.
  • Developing methods for capturing and summarizing team discussions into structured outcomes.
  • Studying ethical, organizational, and social implications of AI‑mediated group communication.

Outcomes so far

  • Prototype agents for facilitation, retrieval‑augmented generation (RAG), and structured note‑taking.
  • Preliminary metrics for participation equity and outcome capture fidelity in group chat.
  • Design patterns for knowledge graph‑assisted context routing in team conversations.
  • Field insights from controlled deployments with research collaborators.

Team

The outcome is developed collaboratively by researchers from Tampere University, University of Helsinki, and University of Jyväskylä, combining expertise in machine learning, organizational communication, and human‑computer interaction.
