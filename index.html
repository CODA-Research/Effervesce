Effervesce: an outcome of the SODA & GET:ORG research projects

Developed collaboratively by Tampere University, University of Helsinki, and University of Jyväskylä, Effervesce investigates how multi‑agent LLMs can facilitate balanced participation, knowledge grounding, and decision capture in digital workspaces.

About this research outcome

Effervesce is a tangible outcome emerging from research on AI‑mediated organizational communication. It demonstrates how multi‑agent LLMs can support multi‑party conversations in Slack and Microsoft Teams by coordinating facilitation, retrieval, and note‑taking.

Lineage: projects & context

  • SODA — Social Data Science and Digitalization: blogs.helsinki.fi/sodaproject/
  • GET:ORG — Generative Technologies in Communicative Organizing: researchportal.helsinki.fi/.../getorg

Effervesce is not a product; it is a research outcome illustrating methods, tooling, and evaluation practices from these projects.

Effervesce versions

The work has advanced in phases, from initial prototypes to broader ambitions:

Effervesce 1.0

A first version demonstrating Slack integration, webhook processing, and response generation guided by LLMs.

Effervesce 2.0

Extended with multiple agents for sensing, deciding, and communicating, using vector databases, web search, and tool APIs.

Towards a communicative multi‑agent framework

The ongoing plan is to generalize into a broader communicative multi‑agent system for digital teamwork — beyond Slack/Teams, towards reusable architectures for organizational communication.

Research approach

We explore multi‑agent orchestration, retrieval‑augmented dialogue, and organizational memory through knowledge graphs. Evaluation involves workshops, user studies, and benchmarks for turn‑taking, participation equity, and decision fidelity.

Outcomes so far

  • Prototype agents for facilitation, retrieval‑augmented generation (RAG), and structured note‑taking.
  • Preliminary metrics for participation equity and outcome capture fidelity in group chat.
  • Design patterns for knowledge graph‑assisted context routing in team conversations.
  • Field insights from controlled deployments with research collaborators.

Versions & roadmap

Effervesce 1.0

Single‑agent core with tool calling and RAG over channel context; meeting summaries and action extraction.

Effervesce 2.0

Multi‑agent prototype: intent recognition, turn‑taking, context agent, response agent, specialized schedulers; orchestration via a control bus.

Toward a general communicative multi‑agent system

Planned extension for broader organizational communication: reusable agent roles, policy guardrails, and pluggable tools/APIs across platforms.
